× Daniel McNulty, Susane Calegari, Joanna Majsak and Michał Oszmaniec

A Simple and Efficient Joint Measurement Strategy for Estimating Non-Commuting Fermionic Observables and Hamiltonians

We propose a simple scheme to estimate fermionic observables and Hamiltonians relevant in quantum chemistry and correlated fermionic systems. Our approach is based on implementing a joint measurement of a modified (noisy) collection of Majorana monomials of degree-2k in an n mode fermionic system, whose outcome statistics can be reproduced from the (parent) measurement together with an efficient classical post-processing. To realize our measurement we use: (i) a randomization over a set of unitaries that realize products of Majorana fermion operators; (ii) a unitary, sampled at random from a constant-size set of suitably chosen fermionic Gaussian unitaries; (iii) a measurement of fermionic occupation numbers; (iv) suitable post-processing. For any constant k, our scheme can estimate expectation values of all degree-2k Majorana monomials to ε precision using O(nklog(n)/ε2) measurement rounds, matching the performance offered by fermionic shadow tomography. Furthermore, we prove that our joint measurement is asymptotically optimal, in the sense that no parent POVM exists that reduces the sample complexity by more than a constant factor. In certain settings, such as a rectangular lattice of qubits realizing an n mode fermionic system via the Jordan-Wigner transformation, our parent measurement for Majorana pairs and quadruples can be implemented in circuit depth O(n1/2) with O(n3/2) two-qubit gates, offering an improvement over fermionic and matchgate classical shadows that require depth O(n) and O(n2) two-qubit gates. We also benchmark our method on molecular Hamiltonians and observe performances comparable to those offered by fermionic classical shadows.

× Alejandro Díaz-Caro, Gilles Dowek and Octavio Malherbe

From linear logic to quantum control

This talk proposal explores several recent papers by the authors in the quest for a quantum logic founded on the Curry-Howard-Lambek correspondence. We stand in the quantum control paradigm, where the control flow of programs can follow a quantum particle. Our starting point is intuitionistic multiplicative additive linear logic (IMALL) with an extended proof language, the L-S-calculus, allowing to represent matrices, vectors, and measurement-free quantum programs. We then introduce a new logical connective, sup, to express superposition and measurement. Finally, we model this non-deterministic proof-language for IMALL in the category of semimodules over a semiring.
